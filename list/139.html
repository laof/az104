Question : 99 Topic: 2

You have an Azure subscription named Sub1 that contains the resources shown in the following table.

You create a user named Admin1.
To what can you add Admin1 as a co-administrator?

  • A..RG1
  • B..MG1
  • CorrectC..Sub1
  • D..VM1
Suggested Answer:

Correct Answer:

C. Sub1
You can add Admin1 as a co-administrator at the subscription level (Sub1). Co-administrators have the same access as the service administrator, except they cannot change the service administrator. This role is managed through the Azure classic deployment model.
-----------------

Explanation of Other Options:

A. RG1 is a resource group, and while you can assign RBAC roles at this level, the co-administrator role is not applicable at the resource group level.
B. MG1 is a management group, which is used for managing access and policies across multiple subscriptions, but the co-administrator role does not apply here.
D. VM1 is a virtual machine, and you can assign RBAC roles to it, but not the co-administrator role, which is only applicable at the subscription level.

Brief Description of Co-Administrator Role:

The co-administrator role in Azure is a legacy role from the classic deployment model. A co-administrator has the same administrative privileges as the service administrator, except they cannot change the service administrator. This role allows full management of all resources in the subscription and is assigned only at the subscription level. It is managed through the Azure Account Center or classic portal, not through RBAC.
