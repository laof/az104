Question : 49 Topic: 3

HOTSPOT

-
You have an Azure subscription. The subscription contains a storage account named storage1 that has the lifecycle management rules shown in the following table.
On June 1, you store two blobs in storage1 as shown in the following table.
For each of the following statements, select Yes if the statement is true. Otherwise, select No.
NOTE: Each correct selection is worth one point.

Suggested Answer:
Statement 1: On June 6, File1 will be stored in the Cool access tier. No
Statement 2: On June 1, File2 will be stored in the Cool access tier. No
Statement 3: On June 16, File2 will be stored in the Archive access tier. No
Explanations of right options:
Statement 1: No: File1 is in container1. Rule1 moves it to Archive after 3 days (June 4), and Rule2 moves blobs older than 5 days to Cool storage, but by June 6, File1 has already been moved to Archive by Rule1. So, File1 will not be in the Cool tier on June 6.
Statement 2: No: File2 is in container2 and starts in the Hot tier on June 1; there is no rule to move it to Cool on June 1. No lifecycle management rule applies to move it to Cool on that day.
Statement 3: No: File2 is in container2. Rule3 will delete File2 after 10 days (June 11), so by June 16, File2 will have already been deleted and cannot be in the Archive tier.
-----------------
Statement 1: Yes: Incorrect. File1 is moved to Archive on June 4 and cannot be in the Cool tier on June 6.
Statement 2: Yes: Incorrect. There is no rule that moves File2 to the Cool tier on June 1.
Statement 3: Yes: Incorrect. File2 is deleted on June 11, so it cannot be moved to Archive on June 16.

Additional:

- If you define more than one action on the same blob, lifecycle management applies the least expensive action to the blob. For example, action delete is cheaper than action tierToArchive. Action tierToArchive is cheaper than action tierToCool.
- If no prefix match is specified, then the policy will apply to all the blobs in the storage account.
