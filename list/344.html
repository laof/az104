Question : 92 Topic: 4

HOTSPOT

-
You need to configure a new Azure App Service app named WebApp1. The solution must meet the following requirements:
• WebApp1 must be able to verify a custom domain name of app.contoso.com.
• WebApp1 must be able to automatically scale up to eight instances.
• Costs and administrative effort must be minimized.
Which pricing plan should you choose, and which type of record should you use to verify the domain? To answer, select the appropriate options in the answer area.
NOTE: Each correct answer is worth one point.

Suggested Answer:
1. Standard
2. TXT
-----------------
Explanation of Correct Options:
Standard: The Standard App Service plan allows you to scale up to 10 instances and supports custom domains, which is required to meet the scaling and verification needs. Basic, Free, and Shared plans do not support all these features.
TXT: Adding a TXT record is the recommended and standard way to verify a custom domain name for an Azure App Service.
Explanation of Other Options:
Basic: The Basic plan supports scaling but does not support all custom domain features and is less commonly used for production with custom domain verification requirements.
Free: The Free plan does not support scaling or custom domains.
Shared: The Shared plan also does not support custom domains or scaling to multiple instances.
A, AAAA, PTR: These record types are not used for domain verification with Azure App Service; TXT is required for domain verification.
Additional:
- Auto-scaling is a feature that is available in the Standard, Premium, and Isolated tiers.
- To verify a domain, Azure uses TXT and MX record
