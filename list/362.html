Question : 110 Topic: 4

You have an Azure subscription that contains a container group named Group1. Group1 contains two Azure container instances as shown in the following table.

You need to ensure that container2 can use CPU resources without negatively affecting container1.
What should you do?

  • A..Increase the resource limit of container1 to three CPUs.
  • B..Increase the resource limit of container2 to six CPUs.
  • CorrectC..Remove the resource limit for both containers.
  • D..Decrease the resource limit of container2 to two CPUs.
Suggested Answer:
D..Decrease the resource limit of container2 to two CPUs.
-----------------
Explanation of right option:
D..Decrease the resource limit of container2 to two CPUs — By setting the resource limit of container2 to two CPUs (equal to or less than its resource request), you ensure container2 does not exceed its fair share, preventing it from consuming resources that could impact container1. This way, both containers will have their CPU usage capped and will not affect each other.
Other options explained:
A..Increase the resource limit of container1 to three CPUs — This allows container1 to use more CPU, but does not prevent container2 from potentially using more than container1, which could still cause contention.
B..Increase the resource limit of container2 to six CPUs — This could allow container2 to consume even more CPU and negatively impact container1.
C..Remove the resource limit for both containers — Without resource limits, either container could use all available CPU, leading to resource contention and possible negative impact on container1.
