You have an Azure subscription that contains 10 virtual networks. The virtual networks are hosted in separate resource groups.
Another administrator plans to create several network security groups (NSGs) in the subscription.
You need to ensure that when an NSG is created, it automatically blocks TCP port 8080 between the virtual networks.
Solution: You assign a built-in policy definition to the subscription.
Does this meet the goal?

  • A..Yes
  • CorrectB..No
Suggested Answer:
B.. No
-----------------
Explanation:
Assigning a built-in policy definition can enforce that NSGs are created or configured a certain way, but Azure built-in policies do not natively block specific ports (such as TCP 8080) between virtual networks. Policies can require that NSGs exist or that certain rules are present, but they cannot directly enforce the block of traffic by themselvesâ€”they require you to specify a policy that audits or denies the absence of such a rule, not the blocking itself.
Therefore, this solution does not meet the goal.
