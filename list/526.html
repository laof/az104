You have the Azure virtual machines shown in the following table:

You have a Recovery Services vault that protects VM1 and VM2.
You need to protect VM3 and VM4 by using Recovery Services.
What should you do first?

  • CorrectA..Create a new Recovery Services vault
  • B..Create a storage account
  • C..Configure the extensions for VM3 and VM4
  • D..Create a new backup policy
Suggested Answer:
A..Create a new Recovery Services vault
-----------------

Explanation of Right Options

A..Create a new Recovery Services vault — Recovery Services vaults are regional resources. You cannot use a Recovery Services vault in one region (West Europe) to back up VMs in another region (North Europe). To protect VM3 and VM4, which are in North Europe, you must first create a new Recovery Services vault in that same region.

Explanation of Other Options

B..Create a storage account — You do not need to manually create a storage account for Recovery Services vault; it manages storage automatically.
C..Configure the extensions for VM3 and VM4 — Extensions are configured as part of enabling backup, but you must have a vault in the correct region first.
D..Create a new backup policy — Creating a backup policy is required, but only after you have a vault in the correct region.
