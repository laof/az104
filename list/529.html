HOTSPOT -

You have an Azure virtual machine named VM1 and a Recovery Services vault named Vault1.
You create a backup policy named Policy1 as shown in the exhibit. (Click the Exhibit tab.)
You configure the backup of VM1 to use Policy1 on Thursday, January 1 at 1:00 AM.
You need to identify the number of available recovery points for VM1.
How many recovery points are available on January 8 and January 15? To answer, select the appropriate options in the answer area.
NOTE: Each correct selection is worth one point.
Hot Area:

Suggested Answer:
statement1: 6
statement2: 8

Explanation of Right Options

For the first date, January 8 at 2:00 PM, the correct answer (based on the available options) is Option B: 6.
This reflects 5 latest daily recovery points plus 1 monthly recovery point, since the actual correct count (7, with 1 weekly as well) is not listed among the options.
For the second date, January 15 at 2:00 PM, the correct answer (based on the available options) is Option C: 8.
This reflects 5 latest daily recovery points, 2 weekly recovery points, and 1 monthly recovery point. The actual correct count is 9 (including 1 yearly), but 9 is not provided, so 8 is the closest correct choice.
-----------------

Explanation of Other Options

Option A: 5 does not account for the additional retention types and only considers the daily recovery points, which underestimates the total recovery points available. Option D: 9 overcounts for the first date, as it includes more recovery points than possible by that time, and for the second date, would be correct if yearly retention is included, but since 9 is not available for the first question, it cannot be chosen. Option C: 8 for the first date is an overestimate, as only 6 total recovery points are available by January 8 (5 daily + 1 monthly). Option B: 6 for the second date is an underestimate, as it misses weekly and monthly/yearly points.
