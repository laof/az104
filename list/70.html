Question : 30 Topic: 2

You have an Azure subscription linked to an Azure Active Directory tenant. The tenant includes a user account named User1.

You need to ensure that User1 can assign a policy to the tenant root management group.
What should you do?

  • A..Assign the Owner role for the Azure Subscription to User1, and then modify the default conditional access policies.
  • B..Assign the Owner role for the Azure subscription to User1, and then instruct User1 to configure access management for Azure resources.
  • CorrectC..Assign the Global administrator role to User1, and then instruct User1 to configure access management for Azure resources.
  • D..Create a new management group and delegate User1 as the owner of the new management group.
Suggested Answer:

Question 2-30

Correct Answer: C..Assign the Global administrator role to User1, and then instruct User1 to configure access management for Azure resources.
Explanation: To assign a policy at the tenant root management group level, the user must have access to manage that scope. By default, only users with the Global administrator role in Azure AD can elevate their access to manage all Azure resources, including the tenant root group. Once User1 is a Global admin, they can enable Access management for Azure resources in Azure AD, which allows them to assign policies at the root level.
-----------------
A..Assign the Owner role for the Azure Subscription to User1, and then modify the default conditional access policies.: Incorrect. The Owner role at the subscription level does not grant permissions at the tenant root management group level.
B..Assign the Owner role for the Azure subscription to User1, and then instruct User1 to configure access management for Azure resources.: Incorrect. This still limits User1 to the subscription scope, not the tenant root.
C..Assign the Global administrator role to User1, and then instruct User1 to configure access management for Azure resources.: Correct. This allows User1 to elevate their access and manage policies at the tenant root level.
D..Create a new management group and delegate User1 as the owner of the new management group.: Incorrect. This only gives User1 access to the new group, not the tenant root management group.
